What would you do in our team

  • Have a lot of fun while engaging in cutting edge research
  • Learn some of the coolest mathematical, computational and experimental techniques in ecohydrology
  • Publish your research in peer-reviewed technical journals
  • Present your work in national and international conferences

Things to consider before joining our team:

  • If you are thinking about getting a PhD, read this article by Lucy Taylor from University of Oxford.
  • Derrik Brown from Lehigh university has a helpful document for aspiring MS and PhD students to bridge the gap between expectations of you and your advisor.

Include following things in your mail:

  • Resume
  • Transcripts from 12th grade to highest degree
  • Research and/or professional experience
  • Research statement (not more than 2 pages) describing your goals, aspirations and interests
  • List of accomplishments (publications, patents, significant project work, experiments, and computational program development)
